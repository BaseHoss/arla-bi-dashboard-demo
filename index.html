Use case 1 · test

Did Ramadan media spend move volume, or just price and mix?

Objective — confirm the Feb–Mar media step-up drove real demand, not just value inflation.
1. Set media view to Split by channel — note which channels spiked in Feb–Mar.
2. Switch the line Sales value → Sales volume. Volume rising with spend = media working; flat volume while value climbs = price-led.
3. Switch the line to Price per kg. A rising price over the window would explain a value/volume gap.
4. Cross-check MTD / YTD / MAT — MTD should show the sharpest lift if Ramadan timing is captured.
✓ Good: volume & value move together in the spike; price per kg stays roughly flat.▲ Red flag: value up, volume flat, price up — a price-driven illusion of media effectiveness.

Use case 2 · test

Is softer YoY growth a distribution problem or a brand problem?

Objective — trace a YoY growth shortfall back to retail execution vs weakening brand health.
1. Cycle the KPI through Awareness, Consideration, Perception, Salience, Brand communication. Note any KPI flat/declining while spend rises.
2. Check Retail sell-out — shortfall in a few retailers or spread evenly? Compare online (Noon, Talabat) vs in-store.
3. Check Promo vs non-promo — rising promo share with soft growth = volume bought through discounting.
4. On the SKU table, filter for OOS risk / heavy promo — a stock-out on a fast SKU can explain a soft month alone.
✓ Good: KPIs steady, promo share stable, no OOS on top SKUs — a demand/distribution gap to investigate.▲ Red flag: KPIs also softening — the fix is upstream in messaging/media, not merchandising.

Use case 3 · test

How long does a brand communication uplift take to reflect in sales?

Objective — quantify the lag between a brand communication uplift and the sales uplift (the brand payback window).
1. Set the KPI to Brand communication and find the sharpest step-up (an inflection, not drift). Record its month.
2. On sales trend, set the line to Sales value, then Sales volume, and find the month sales visibly accelerates.
3. Count the months between the comms inflection and the sales inflection — the observed lag.
4. Set sales media view to Split by channel — check whether a paid push (TV/Social) is driving a faster, separate response.
✓ Good: a consistent, repeatable lag (e.g. 2–3 months) across cycles — the brand payback window.▲ Red flag: no pattern — sales moves independently, or only alongside paid activation.
